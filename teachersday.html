TEACHERS' DAY 2025

This year's teachers' day celebration was very precious and memorable for me. I learned that our teachers really do deserve celebration, appreciation, and recognition for all their hard work and efforts that most students don't see, including me. I learned to be more appreciative and more aware that they are humans too. They go through highs and lows, goods and bads, or bests and worsts. With this lesson I learned, I will be more thankful for their efforts and even their presence itself, because you never know what they truly feel and went through just to be present in school and teach. During the teacher's day celebration, I actively participated by leading a short zumba dance with the whole class, along with my dance partner. In addition, I also shared to the teachers a letter I made and offered, on behalf of our section. Unexpectedly, while reading the letter halfway, I immediately felt emotional and cried while reading it to them. What also made me cry is that I saw the teachers, whom I'm reading my letter for, cry and wipe their eyes with tissues. I also participated in some games during the program! If I were to share this to others, I'd explain that teachers' day is not just about fun and celebration, it's actually an offering that we offer to our wonderful, mighty teachers who were always there for us. With this, it's time we gave them back what they always deserved. Having these events is crucial as it strengthens the bond between teachers and students and creates a positive and thankful school environment, reminding us that education is a partnership built on kindness, respect, and the desire to help one another grow.
